In 1A we are introduced to the very fundementals of engineering. We learned the necessary math and chemistry for more advanced courses, as well as the basics of programming.

In MTE 100 we were introduced to mechatronics, as well as engineering graphics using both freehand sketching as well as CAD. The pictures Lego robot was my first introduction to an engineering project - a robot that could drive around and shoot ping pong balls into cups.

CHE 102: Chemistry for Engineers
GENE 121: Digital Computation
MATH 115: Linear Algebra for Engineering
MATH 116: Calculus 1 for Engineering
MTE 100: Mechatronics Engineering

Main skills gained:

C++ | Algorithm Design | Data Structure Implementation


In 1B we learn more fundementals, while delving into more applicable engineering applications. We continue with fundementals in calculus, materials and statics, while MTE 140 and MTE 120 teach more useful skills in software theory and hardware.

In Data Structures and Algorithms we had several projects, each involving implementing different abstract data types and writing algorithms for their member functions.

MATH 118: Calculus 2 for Engineering
MTE 111: Structure and Property of Materials
MTE 119: Statics
MTE 120: Circuits
MTE 140: Data Structures and Algorithms

Main skills gained:

PLC | VHDL | Assembly | Structural Failure Analysis


In 2A, while some courses are more core courses that are required in order to be prepared for future courses, the most applicable courses are MTE 262 and MTE 219. In microprocessors and digital logic we are introduced to low level programming, an integral part of the mechatronics program. We were introduced to many aspects of digital logic, applying this using VHDL, PLC and Assembly.

Mechanics of Deformable Solids is an important course introducing us to mechanics in a much more realistic situation than past statics courses. It is the an important stage in introducing us to mechanical analysis.

Main skills gained: PLC, VHDL, Assembly, structural failure analysis.

MTE 201: Experimental Measurement and Statistical Analysis
MTE 202: Ordinary Differential Equations
MTE 219: Mechanics of Deformable Solids
MTE 262: Microprocessors and Digital Logic
SYDE 182: Physics 2 (Dynamics)

Main skills gained:

C | Multithreading | MatLab | Signal Conditioning Circuit Design

2B is a highly challenging term with several courses that have proved useful in real work environments. The high concentration of large projects forces students to manage their time, as well as work efficiently in teams and individually.

MTE 241 is an excellent course for low level programming in real time systems. The projects included coding memory allocation, a multithreaded quick sort algorithm and a basic game. MTE 220 teaches design of signal conditioning circuits, while SYDE 252 teaches the use of software to process signals.

MTE 203: Advanced Calculus
MTE 204: Numerical Methods
MTE 220: Sensors and Instrumentation
MTE 241: Introduction to Computer Structures and Real-Time Systems
SYDE 252: Linear Systems and Signals
